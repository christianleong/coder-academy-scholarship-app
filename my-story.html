Hi, I'm Christian.
This is my story of pursuing my passion and not giving up.

Petronas Twin Tower, Malaysia

My love for technology began at the tender age of one. I was drawn by the sound of keystrokes and the vivid images that came to life on the screen.

Unfortunately, growing up in Malaysia, opportunities to explore this passion were limited. Our education system emphasised traditional subjects like math and science, focusing on what was deemed essential for a successful career.

I moved to Australia when I was 18 and enrolled in a Commerce degree to become an accountant, as I was told it would be a solid choice. Little did I know, my passion wasnt in accounting.

After 5 years as a Chartered Accountant, I thought I had finally made it. But over time I delved deep into my feelings and knew this wasnt my true passion.

It took an episode of burnout, where work dominated my life in an unhealthy way, negatively affecting my physical and mental health.

This led me to make a choice to take a leap of faith and pursue my passion in tech. I was ready to finally pursue this path for myself!

Despite graduating from the software engineering bootcamp at General Assembly, it has been a challenging time in the job market. I painfully discovered that work experience and further upskilling is essential in order to stand out.

I reached out to numerous Coder Academy alumni to hear their stories pursuing the course. It was inspiring for me to hear their positive feedback about their experience and advice to support my decision.

Applying to Coder Academy is my second chance for myself, to try again, to keep upskilling, networking, building projects and not give up seeking my passion, my ikigai.
